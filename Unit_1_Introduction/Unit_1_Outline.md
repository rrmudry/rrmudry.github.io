# Unit 1: Foundations & Measurement

**Pacing:** 2 Weeks (10 Days)  
**Target Audience:** High School Students (Physics of the Universe / CA NGSS)  
**Core Materials:** Rulers, non-standard measurement items (markers, paperclips), meter tapes, electronic scales/balances, blocks of regular shapes (wood, metal), graduated cylinders, irregular stones, stopwatches.

---

## Week 1: Metric Blueprint & The Language of SI
**Goal:** Establish why standard systems are mandatory for science and construct a shared vocabulary of base units, prefixes, conversions, and uncertainty.

### Day 1: Why Standardize? Non-Standard Unit Challenge
*   **Focus:** Core calibration concept. The scientific necessity of shared standards.
*   **Activity:** Physical calibration lab. Students design unique measuring tools using everyday items, experience high-variance data discrepancies, and resolve them by converting to standard metric units.
*   **Essential Question:** Why is a universal standard for measurement essential for science and global collaboration?
*   **Links:** [Student Instructions](https://docs.google.com/document/d/188xprYNY0eogCYww45he7McWXoHCAKxVZZWibX7KaPU/edit?usp=sharing), [Lesson Plan](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1JMY5nWw3dZ_hrxVKjLZisXVsBmV_fQf317EwaEZiS_U/edit?usp=sharing)

### Day 2: Making the Standard & The Language of SI
*   **Focus:** Metric system structure and prefix hierarchy.
*   **Activity:** The Meter Stick Challenge. Constructing and calibrating paper strips into sub-divided measuring tools, followed by formalized notes on the 7 SI base units.
*   **Essential Question:** How do prefix systems expand our capability to mathematically model micro and macro realities?

### Day 3: Unit Conversions
*   **Focus:** Dimensional analysis factor-label cancellation rules.
*   **Activity:** Matrix setup check-ins. Guided "I do, We do, You do" practice solving single and multi-step unit conversions (e.g., converting km/h to m/s).
*   **Essential Question:** How does dimensional analysis safeguard mathematical models across different dimensional levels?
*   **Links:** [Practice Worksheet](https://docs.google.com/document/d/19L0sKqt97x8hOCWEDIpL3pkUmgLGjwcYPnmkPXi8wnM/edit?usp=sharing)

### Day 4: Quality of Measurement
*   **Focus:** Accuracy, precision, and tool resolution boundaries.
*   **Activity:** Density block testing and precision analysis. Sandbox modeling comparing standard rulers with caliper resolution increments.
*   **Essential Question:** What is the critical scientific boundary separating a precise measurement from an accurate one?

### Day 5: Practice & Check-In
*   **Focus:** Mid-unit skill alignment.
*   **Activity:** Station rotation diagnostic check-in and formative assessment covering conversions and sig-fig calculations.
*   **Essential Question:** How do scientific figures communicate the intrinsic resolution of our measurement instruments?

## Week 2: Derived Quantities & Experimental Architecture
**Goal:** Apply base measurements to compound physical realities, design indirect measurement models, and build robust arguments on global scientific systems.

### Day 6: Derived Units
*   **Focus:** Synthesis of compound units.
*   **Activity:** Measuring and calculating density for symmetrical blocks of known materials, followed by rounding justifications.
*   **Essential Question:** How do mass and dimensional constraints synthesize to describe derived physical qualities like density?

### Day 7: Complex Measurement Lab
*   **Focus:** Irregular shapes and fluid displacement designs.
*   **Activity:** "Find the Density of a Rock" lab. Students engineer a customized procedure utilizing graduated cylinder displacements and document replicable blueprints.
*   **Essential Question:** How can we design indirect physical indicators to quantify highly irregular geometric objects?

### Day 8: Experimental Design
*   **Focus:** Isolating constant values and variable controls.
*   **Activity:** Gravitational acceleration design draft. Teams draft experimental procedures to determine the constant "g" using limited tools.
*   **Essential Question:** How can simple measurement constraints isolate grand gravitational constants?

### Day 9: The "Why" of the SI System
*   **Focus:** Industrial engineering risks and metric-mismatch analysis.
*   **Activity:** Class debate and CER argumentation writing based on famous historical aerospace failures due to unit errors.
*   **Essential Question:** What are the cultural, economic, and scientific costs of a fragmented measurement framework?

### Day 10: Unit Review & Assessment
*   **Focus:** Summative performance audit.
*   **Activity:** Exam review circle and comprehensive unit assessment spanning DOK 1-3 problem solving and DOK 4 design prompts.
*   **Essential Question:** How do our measurement tools shape the accuracy of our scientific theories?
