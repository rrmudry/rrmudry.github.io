Unit Overview

This unit is designed for a high school physics course to support students working below grade level. The goal is to build a strong conceptual and practical understanding of speed, velocity, and motion graphs, culminating in the ability to solve DOK 1-4 level problems.

Daily Lesson Outlines

Day 1

Intro to Motion

Objective: Define motion, distance, time, and use ùë£ = ùë•/ùë° to calculate speed.

  • Engage: Watch Day 1 video & discuss
  • Direct Instruction on vocab & formula
  • Guided Practice: Calculate walking speed
  • Exit Ticket on DOK 1 questions

Day 2

"Monster's Journey" Lab

Objective: Collect data in trials and calculate average speed.

  • Warm-up calculation
  • Lab Intro & assign group roles
  • Lab Activity: 3 trials
  • Closing discussion on averages

Day 3

Graphing Motion

Objective: Create and interpret a simple distance vs. time graph.

  • Direct instruction on graph conventions
  • Interactive practice with web activity
  • Exit Ticket: Sketch a graph

Day 4

"Marble Race" Lab

Objective: Investigate how ramp slope affects speed.

  • Warm-up: "Steeper slope = faster speed"
  • Lab Intro & student hypothesis
  • Lab Activity: Data collection

Day 5

Mid-Unit Assessment

Objective: Assess DOK 1 & 2 skills on speed calculation and graphing.

  • Brief Q&A Review
  • 35-minute independent quiz

Day 6

Speed vs. Velocity

Objective: Differentiate between speed (scalar) and velocity (vector).

  • Engage: Importance of direction
  • Direct Instruction with number line
  • Guided Practice: Card sort activity

Day 7

Interpreting Graphs

Objective: Describe motion by analyzing graph slope.

  • Direct Instruction: Positive, negative, zero slope
  • "Graph Story" partner activity

Day 8

Solving DOK 3 Problems

Objective: Apply knowledge to plan and solve multi-step problems.

  • Model problem breakdown (e.g., Ben & Maria)
  • Group work on posters for DOK 3 problems

Day 9

Unit Review

Objective: Solidify all unit concepts for final assessment.

  • Gallery Walk of DOK 3 posters
  • Whole-class review game (Kahoot!, etc.)

Day 10

Final Assessment

Objective: Assess mastery of all unit objectives (DOK 1-3).

  • Brief Q&A Review
  • 40-minute independent test

Assessments

Mid-Unit Quiz (Day 5)

A 35-minute quiz covering DOK 1 and DOK 2 concepts to check foundational understanding.

Content:

  • Simple calculations for speed (ùë£ = ùë•/ùë°)
  • Creating a distance-time graph from a data table
  • Interpreting a simple pre-made graph

Sample Question:

"A toy car travels at a constant velocity of 0.5 m/s. How far will it travel in 10 seconds? Show your work."

Final Unit Test (Day 10)

A 40-minute cumulative test covering DOK 1-3 concepts, assessing both skills and strategic thinking.

Content:

  • Mix of multiple-choice and short-answer
  • Calculations, graph creation, and interpretation
  • One strategic-thinking problem (like DOK 3 list)

Sample Question:

"The distance vs. time graphs for two different cars are plotted on the same axes. Justify which car was moving faster, using specific evidence from the slope of the lines in your explanation."
