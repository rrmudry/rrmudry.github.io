# First Law of Thermodynamics: Adaptive Question Bank

This document tracks the questions for the interactive assessment. Questions are designed to progress from basic simulation navigation to complex application.

## Level 1: Simulation Proficiency (Basics)
*Goal: Ensure students understand the controls, the sign convention, and the relationship between U, Q, and W.*

1. **[Multiple Choice]** You click the "Add Heat" button twice (+100 J). If the piston does not move (W = 0), what should the "ΔU" value on the dashboard read?
   - A) 0 J
   - B) +50 J
   - C) +100 J (Correct)
   - D) -100 J
   - *Explanation: ΔU = Q + W. If Q = 100 and W = 0, then ΔU = 100.*

2. **[True/False]** Compressing the gas (clicking the "Compress" button) results in a positive Work (+W) value on the dashboard.
   - Answer: True
   - *Explanation: Work is positive when work is done ON the system (compression).*

3. **[Multiple Choice]** What happens to the "Internal Energy (U)" readout when you click the "Remove Heat" button?
   - A) It increases
   - B) It decreases (Correct)
   - C) It stays the same
   - D) It fluctuates randomly
   - *Explanation: Removing heat lowers the internal energy of the system.*

4. **[Multiple Choice]** When the gas is allowed to "Expand," what is the sign of the Work (W) shown on the dashboard?
   - A) Positive (+)
   - B) Negative (-) (Correct)
   - C) Neutral (0)
   - *Explanation: Expansion means the gas is doing work on the surroundings, which we define as negative work (-W).*

5. **[Numerical Response]** If you click "Add Heat" (+50 J) and then "Compress" (+50 J), what is the total change in Internal Energy (ΔU)?
   - Answer: 100
   - *Explanation: ΔU = 50 (Q) + 50 (W) = 100 J.*

6. **[Matching]** Match the action to the sign convention:
   - 1. Adding Heat -> A. +Q
   - 2. Removing Heat -> B. -Q
   - 3. Compressing Gas -> C. +W
   - 4. Expanding Gas -> D. -W

## Level 2: Problem Solving (Calculations)
*Goal: Apply the formula ΔU = Q + W to find missing values.*

7. **[Multiple Choice]** A student starts the simulation and adds 200J of heat. To keep the Internal Energy change (ΔU) at exactly 100J, what must the gas do?
   - A) Expand (-100J Work) (Correct)
   - B) Compress (+100J Work)
   - C) Expand (-300J Work)
   - D) Nothing (W = 0)
   - *Explanation: 100 (ΔU) = 200 (Q) + W. Solving for W gives -100 J.*

8. **[Multiple Choice]** The dashboard shows ΔU = -50 J and Q = -150 J. What is the value of W?
   - A) +100 J (Correct)
   - B) -100 J
   - C) +200 J
   - D) -200 J
   - *Explanation: -50 = -150 + W. Adding 150 to both sides gives W = 100 J.*

9. **[True/False]** If a process is "Adiabatic" (meaning Q = 0), then any work done on the gas will be equal to the total change in internal energy.
   - Answer: True
   - *Explanation: If Q=0, then ΔU = W.*

10. **[Numerical Response]** In an isothermal process, the temperature (and thus ΔU) remains 0. If 75 J of heat is added (+Q), how much work (W) must the gas perform? (Enter the negative value)
    - Answer: -75
    - *Explanation: 0 = 75 + W, so W = -75.*

11. **[Multiple Choice]** You observe the particle speed increasing significantly. This visual change corresponds to:
    - A) A decrease in ΔU
    - B) An increase in ΔU (Correct)
    - C) Work being done by the gas
    - D) The system being at absolute zero
    - *Explanation: Internal energy is directly related to the kinetic energy (speed) of the particles.*

12. **[Multiple Choice]** If Q = +500 J and W = -500 J, what is the value of ΔU?
    - A) +1000 J
    - B) -1000 J
    - C) 0 J (Correct)
    - D) +500 J
    - *Explanation: 500 + (-500) = 0.*

13. **[Numerical Response]** The piston is compressed with 120 J of work. 40 J of heat is lost to the environment during the compression. What is the final ΔU?
    - Answer: 80
    - *Explanation: ΔU = 120 (W) - 40 (Q) = 80 J.*

## Level 3: Application & Extension (Real World)
*Goal: Connect the simulation to real-world objects and thermodynamic cycles.*

14. **[Multiple Choice]** (Scenario: Bicycle Pump) When you rapidly pump air into a tire, the pump gets hot. In this scenario, you are doing work ON the gas (+W) very quickly. Because heat doesn't have time to escape, what happens to U?
    - A) U decreases (pump cools)
    - B) U increases (pump heats up) (Correct)
    *Explanation: For a quick stroke (Q ≈ 0), +W leads to +ΔU, raising the temperature.*

15. **[Multiple Choice]** (Scenario: Aerosol Can) When you spray a can of compressed air, the can feels cold. This is because the gas is expanding (-W) rapidly. If Q is nearly zero, why does it feel cold?
    - A) Energy is being added to the can
    - B) Work is being done by the gas, pulling energy from U (Correct)
    *Explanation: ΔU = 0 + (-W). ΔU is negative, so temperature drops.*

16. **[True/False]** If a system is perfectly insulated (Q = 0) and the gas expands against a piston (Work done by the gas), the internal energy of the system MUST decrease.
    - Answer: True
    - *Explanation: In an adiabatic expansion (Q=0), the work is done at the expense of internal energy (ΔU = -W), causing a temperature drop.*

17. **[Numerical Response]** A machine takes in 1000 J of heat and performs 300 J of work. How much "waste heat" is exhausted back into the environment?
    - Answer: 700
    - *Explanation: 1000 total in = 300 out as work + 700 out as wasted heat.*

18. **[Multiple Choice]** Which of these is an example of an "Isochoric" process (Work = 0)?
    - A) A balloon expanding in the sun
    - B) Heating a rigid steel tank of gas (Correct)
    - C) A piston moving in a car engine
    - D) Squeezing a sponge
    - *Explanation: Isochoric means "constant volume," so no work is done.*

    - 1. System gains heat (Add Heat) -> A. +Q
    - 2. System loses heat (Remove Heat) -> B. -Q
    - 3. Piston compresses gas -> C. +W
    - 4. Gas expands piston -> D. -W

20. **[Multiple Choice]** If you want to increase the pressure in the cylinder as much as possible with only TWO clicks, which combination is best?
    - A) Add Heat + Expand
    - B) Add Heat + Compress (Correct)
    - C) Remove Heat + Compress
    - D) Remove Heat + Expand
    - *Explanation: Pressure increases with heat and with decreasing volume (compression).*
