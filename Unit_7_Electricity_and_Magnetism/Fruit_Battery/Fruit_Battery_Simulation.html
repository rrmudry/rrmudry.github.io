🧪 Chemistry & Physics Breakdown

Close the circuit to observe the chemical reactions!

Anode (Oxidation) Left Side

Zinc has a lower reduction potential, meaning it wants to give up electrons. It dissolves into the solution as ions.

Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻

Cathode (Reduction) Right Side

Electrons travel through the wire to the Copper electrode. Here, Hydrogen ions (H⁺) from the acid grab the electrons and turn into Hydrogen gas bubbles!

2H⁺ + 2e⁻ → H₂ (gas)
Observe the movement of particles:
  • Electrons (e⁻): Form during oxidation at the anode, travel up the metal plate, through the wire pathway, across the switch, down the cathode plate, and combine with H⁺ at the active interface.
  • Anode Metal Ions (Zn²⁺): Dissolve off the anode plate and accumulate in the surrounding electrolyte solution, forming a localized region of dissolved positive ions.
  • Acidic Hydronium Ions (H⁺): Migrate directly to the cathode and get reduced by arriving electrons to form bubbles.
  • Hydrogen Gas Bubbles (H₂): Form directly at the cathode plate surface and float straight up to escape.
