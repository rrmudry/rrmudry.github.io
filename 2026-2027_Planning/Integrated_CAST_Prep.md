# CAST Daily Integration Guide: Data & Graphing for Physics

This guide provides a structured approach to integrating California Science Test (CAST) preparation into a high school Physics curriculum. The focus is on Science and Engineering Practices (SEPs), specifically analyzing data and using mathematics, which are the most common stumbling blocks for 11th graders.

---

## 1. The "Weekly Rotation" Strategy (5-Minute Bell-Ringers)

Using a rotating theme keeps the content fresh while ensuring students see Earth, Life, and Physical science contexts before testing day.

| Day | Theme | CAST Domain | Skill Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mon** | Pattern Hunt | Earth/Space | Trend Recognition: Is it linear, cyclical, or exponential? |
| **Tue** | Table to Tech | Engineering | Translation: Sketching a graph from 3-4 data points. |
| **Wed** | "What's Wrong?" | Life Science | Error Analysis: Finding swapped axes or broken scales. |
| **Thu** | The Predictor | Physics | Extrapolation: What happens after the last data point? |
| **Fri** | Claim-Evidence | Environmental | Argumentation: Connecting a data trend to a written claim. |

## 2. Activity Deep-Dives

### A. The "Mystery Axis" (3 mins)
- **The Setup:** Project a graph with a clear curve (e.g., an S-curve or an inverse relationship) but erase the labels.
- **The Task:** "If this were a Biology graph, what could the Y-axis be? What if it were a Physics graph?"
- **Physics Connection:** Use a $1/x$ curve. In Physics, it's Newton’s Law of Gravitation ($F$ vs $r$). In Earth Science, it’s Atmosphere Density vs Altitude.

### B. "Data Translation" (5-Minute Sketches)
- **The Setup:** Provide a tiny 3-row table.
    - *Example:* (Light Intensity: 10, 20, 40) | (Photosynthesis Rate: 5, 10, 20).
- **The Task:** Students have 60 seconds to draw a "Low-Fidelity Sketch" (no rulers, just the shape).
- **Why it works:** It stops the "pixel-peeping" anxiety and helps them identify the function of the data.

### C. The "Distractor Analysis"
- **The Setup:** Show a CAST sample question with a complex multi-line graph.
- **The Task:** "Identify the two answers that are mathematically impossible based on the slope of this line."
- **Why it works:** Many students guess randomly on the CAST. This teaches them to use the provided data as a "filter" to eliminate decoys.

## 3. High-Value Cross-Curricular Topics for Physics

You can hit "Earth and Life Science" standards while still teaching Physics math:

- **Seismic Waves (Earth Science):** Use $v = f\lambda$ and $v = d/t$ to calculate the distance to an earthquake epicenter. It teaches P and S wave behavior (CAST standard) while practicing kinematics.
- **Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) Diagrams (Space Science):** These graphs are essentially "Luminosity vs. Temperature." Use them when teaching Thermodynamics or Blackbody Radiation.
- **Energy Pyramids (Life Science):** When teaching the Law of Conservation of Energy, show a biological energy pyramid. Ask: "Where does the 'lost' 90% of energy go?" (Answer: Heat/Entropy).
- **Climate Feedback Loops (Earth Science):** Use these when teaching Vectors or Systems. A positive feedback loop is just a system where the output reinforces the input ($+\Delta$ leading to more $+\Delta$).

## 4. CAST "Interface" Simulation

The digital nature of the test is often what trips students up. Mimic these interactions in class:

- **Drag-and-Drop:** Use printed cards. Have students "drag" (place) a label like "Inertia" or "Net Force = 0" onto the correct section of a velocity-time graph.
- **Hot Spots:** During a slide deck, have a student come to the board and circle the "point of maximum potential energy" on a graph.
- **The "Desmos" Factor:** Since CAST uses the Desmos calculator, ensure students are comfortable using it to find slopes ($y = mx + b$) rather than just doing it by hand.

## 5. Quick Examples for Next Week

- **Monday (Pattern Hunt):** Graph of Carbon Dioxide levels over 50 years. *Question:* Why is there a "zigzag" every year? (Seasonal variation).
- **Tuesday (Table to Tech):** Table showing the mass of a star and its lifespan. *Task:* Sketch the relationship.
- **Wednesday ("What's Wrong?"):** A graph showing "Speed vs Time" for a falling object, but the line is perfectly horizontal. *Task:* Explain why this graph is impossible on Earth.
