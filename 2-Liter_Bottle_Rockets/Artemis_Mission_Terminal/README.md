# Artemis Moon Base Blueprint

A creative engineering and design dossier for planning a permanent lunar settlement. This interactive terminal provides students with a high-fidelity "Mission Control" environment to brainstorm, draw, and label their visionary Moon Base designs.

## 🌕 Features

- **Immersive Environment**: Features a full-page, high-resolution background of the Apollo 15 lunar exploration site.
- **Glassmorphism UI**: A futuristic, semi-transparent interface that maintains legibility while keeping the lunar landscape visible.
- **Mission Briefing**: Clear, concise instructions translated into a high-priority dossier format.
- **Visual Inspiration Section**: A dedicated reference card featuring a detailed photograph from the Apollo missions to aid in landscape visualization.
- **Real-Time Mission Clock**: A 50-minute countdown timer with color-coded phases (Brainstorming, Drawing, Polishing) to keep students on task.
- **Blueprint Readiness Checklist**: An interactive rubric that calculates a "Readiness Score" as students complete the 4 mandatory design components.

## 🛠️ The 4 "Must-Haves"

Students must incorporate the following engineering systems into their designs:
1. **Living Quarters**: Habitats where astronauts sleep, eat, and relax.
2. **Power Station**: Sustainable energy sources (Solar, Reactor, Batteries) to power the base.
3. **Rovers & Airlocks**: Transportation for exploration and safe entry/exit systems.
4. **Space Garden**: Hydroponic or greenhouse systems for food production and oxygen recycling.

## 🧪 Educational Objectives

- **Systems Thinking**: Identify the essential components required for human survival in a hostile environment.
- **Technical Drawing**: Practice labeling complex systems and using arrows to show relationships and functionality.
- **Time Management**: Work within a strict 50-minute mission window to move from concept to final design.
- **Creative Problem Solving**: Balance futuristic imagination with realistic lunar constraints (low gravity, lack of atmosphere).

## 💻 Built With

- **HTML5 & Vanilla JavaScript**: Core structure and logic.
- **Tailwind CSS**: Modern utility-first styling and layout.
- **Feather Icons**: Minimalist engineering iconography.
- **Google Fonts**: Space Grotesk and Inter for a high-tech "Mission Dossier" aesthetic.

## 📖 How to Use

1. **Review Dossier**: Read the Mission Briefing and study the Apollo 15 visual reference.
2. **Start Timer**: The 50-minute clock begins automatically on page load.
3. **Brainstorm (0-10m)**: Think about the 4 Must-Haves and wild creative ideas.
4. **Design (10-40m)**: Use blank paper and art supplies to draw and color the Moon Base.
5. **Finalize (40-50m)**: Add labels to all 4 required sections and verify using the **Blueprint Checklist**.
6. **Deploy**: Once the Readiness Score hits 100%, write your name and the base's name at the top of your drawing for submission.
