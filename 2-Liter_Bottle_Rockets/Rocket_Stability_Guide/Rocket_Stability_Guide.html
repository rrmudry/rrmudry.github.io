Rocket Stability Guide

Before you head to the launch pad, you need to prove your rocket will fly straight. Follow these steps to find your centers of mass and pressure.

Flight Condition Required

Perform all of these tests with your rocket completely assembled (fins attached, nose cone on, parachutes packed) but EMPTY of water.

1

Find the Center of Mass (CM)

The Center of Mass is the exact balance point of your rocket. It is the point where gravity pulls equally on the top and bottom halves.

Slide the string until the rocket balances perfectly level.

2

Find the Center of Pressure (CP)

The Center of Pressure is the point where wind forces act on your rocket. We can find it by calculating the geometric center (centroid) of the rocket's 2D silhouette. Hold your camera perfectly steady! First, capture a picture of an empty background. Then, place your rocket in the frame and scan it.

If your rocket is clear or hard to detect automatically, use the Manual Trace tool. Take a photo of your rocket, then click around its edges to create an outline. The tool will calculate the center of that shape.

3

The Swing Test

The Golden Rule of Stability

For a safe flight, your Center of Mass (CM) must be closer to the NOSE than your Center of Pressure (CP).

If your marks look good, let's simulate flight. If the rocket stays pointed forward while being swung, it will stay pointed forward when launched.

Reading the Results:

PASS (Stable):

Nose points forward into the circle as it swings. Ready for launch!

FAIL (Unstable):

Rocket tumbles or flies tail-first. Do not launch! See fixes below.

How to Fix an Unstable Rocket

If your rocket tumbled, you need to make it act more like a dart. Here are the two ways to fix it:

Fix 1: Add Nose Weight

Adding mass to the front pulls your Center of Mass forward. Darts have heavy tips for this exact reason.

Action: Pack modeling clay or washers into the nose cone. Retest CM, then swing test again.

Fix 2: Modify the Fins

Fins act like the feathers on an arrow. If they aren't big enough, they can't keep the back end straight.

Action: Make fins larger or move them further down toward the nozzle. Swing test again.
