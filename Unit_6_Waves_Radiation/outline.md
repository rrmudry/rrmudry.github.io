# Unit 6: Waves and Electromagnetic Radiation Outline
Unit Outline: Waves and Electromagnetic Radiation

Target Grade Level: High School Physics

Estimated Duration: 25 Instructional Days (5 Weeks)

Standard Alignment: NGSS HS-PS4-1 through HS-PS4-5

This unit transitions from the visible, mechanical behavior of energy through matter to the complex behavior of light and its role in modern information technology.

Week 1: Mechanical Wave Fundamentals

Focus: Energy transfer and fundamental math (HS-PS4-1)

Monday: What is a Wave? Introduction to energy vs. matter transfer; Pulse vs. Periodic waves.

Tuesday: Wave Types: Modeling Transverse vs. Longitudinal particle motion using slinkies or simulations.

Wednesday: The Wave Equation ($v = f\lambda$): Practicing calculations for wave speed, frequency, and wavelength.

Thursday: Period and Frequency: Exploring the inverse relationship ($T = 1/f$); Simple harmonic motion basics.

Friday: Week 1 Review & Quiz: Assessment on wave anatomy and basic calculations; turn in week 1 problem sets.

Week 2: Wave Behaviors & Interactions

Focus: Modeling wave behavior at interfaces and with other waves (HS-PS4-3)

Monday: Boundary Behaviors: Qualitative exploration of Reflection (fixed/free end) and Refraction (bending).

Tuesday: Diffraction & Interference: Observing waves bending around obstacles and the Principle of Superposition.

Wednesday: Constructive vs. Destructive Interference: Mapping "dead spots" and "loud spots" in sound and light.

Thursday: Standing Waves & Resonance: The physics of musical instruments, harmonics, and natural frequency.

Friday: Interference Lab/Activity: Hands-on station work with ripple tanks or sound interference apps; turn in Lab reports.

Week 3: The Electromagnetic Spectrum

Focus: Light as a wave and its interaction with matter (HS-PS4-3, HS-PS4-4)

Monday: The Nature of Light: Introduction to EM waves (no medium required) and the constant speed $c$.

Tuesday: The EM Spectrum Map: Categorizing waves from Radio to Gamma by wavelength and frequency.

Wednesday: Energy & Matter: How materials absorb, reflect, or transmit specific frequencies (Transparency vs. Opacity).

Thursday: The Dangers of Radiation: Evaluating the impact of Ionizing Radiation (UV, X-ray, Gamma) on biological tissue.

Friday: EM Spectrum Research Quiz: Identifying regions of the spectrum and their uses; turn in spectrum diagrams/projects.

Week 4: Wave-Particle Duality & Tech

Focus: Evaluating competing models and information transfer (HS-PS4-2, HS-PS4-3, HS-PS4-5)

Monday: Evidence for Waves: Reviewing the Double-Slit experiment and Polarization.

Tuesday: Evidence for Particles: Introduction to the Photoelectric Effect and the concept of "Photons."

Wednesday: Analog vs. Digital: Comparing continuous waves to binary pulses; why digital is superior for storage.

Thursday: Information Technology: How fiber optics, cell phones, and routers encode and transmit information.

Friday: Duality & Tech Assessment: Comparing models of light and digital/analog signals; turn in tech-focus assignments.

Week 5: Application & Summative Assessment

Focus: Integration of concepts and final evaluation

Monday: Real-World Physics: How medical imaging (MRIs/X-rays) and solar cells utilize wave principles.

Tuesday: Review Session Part 1: Mechanical waves and interactions (Weeks 1 & 2).

Wednesday: Review Session Part 2: EM radiation, duality, and technology (Weeks 3 & 4).

Thursday: Unit Summative Exam: Comprehensive assessment of the Waves unit.

Friday: Final Performance Task: Students complete/present their "How Technology Uses Waves" project; Final notebook/work submission.

Cross-Cutting Concepts to Emphasize

Patterns: Using graphs to predict wave behavior.

Cause and Effect: How changing a medium changes speed, and how changing frequency changes energy.

Structure and Function: How the design of an antenna or a telescope is dictated by the wavelength it is meant to capture.
