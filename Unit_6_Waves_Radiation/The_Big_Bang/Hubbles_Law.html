Cosmic Standard Candles

Galaxies are vast oceans of hundreds of billions of stars. From Earth, their light usually blends into a fuzzy, inseparable glow. But occasionally, a few special stars stand out: Cepheid Variables.

Leavitt's Law

These unique stars physically expand and contract, causing them to pulse in brightness. Henrietta Swan Leavitt discovered a critical relationship between them: The longer a Cepheid takes to pulse (its period), the greater its true, intrinsic brightness (its luminosity).

The Cosmic Yardstick

Why does this matter? By timing a star's pulse, we instantly know how bright it actually is. By comparing that true brightness to how dim it appears in our telescope, we can calculate its exact distance from Earth!
