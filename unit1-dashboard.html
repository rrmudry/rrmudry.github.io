Unit Overview

This unit establishes the fundamental language of physics: **measurement**. Students will begin by understanding the critical need for a standardized system of units (SI) and will learn to identify and use base and derived units. They will develop practical skills in unit conversion, scientific notation, and assessing the quality of measurements (accuracy, precision, and significant figures). The unit culminates in students applying these skills to solve complex problems and design their own scientific investigations, building a strong foundation for all future physics topics.

Learning Objectives

DOK 1

Identify the seven SI base units, their quantities, and metric prefixes.

DOK 2

Convert quantities using the dimensional analysis factor-label system; analyze measurements for accuracy and precision.

DOK 3

Evaluate derived quantities and justify mathematical operations using significant figure parameters.

DOK 4

Design procedural experiments to isolate constant values; construct robust, evidence-backed arguments validating the absolute necessity of standardized global unit systems.
